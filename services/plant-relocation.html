Plant Relocation

When the whole floor moves, the order is everything. We relocate full production lines, machine cells, and entire facilities — disconnect, sequenced transport, reinstall, re-level — planned so your line is down only for the move window, not a day longer.

Quick answer: Plant relocation is the sequenced move of an entire facility with minimal downtime. Badass Logistics surveys and tags every asset, sequences the move backward from production, then rigs, hauls, and resets each machine in install order across all 50 states.

One team owns the whole move

A plant move isn't one big lift — it's dozens of moves in the right order. Disconnect, teardown, sequenced transport, reinstall, recommission. Get the sequence wrong and the new floor sits half-built while production bleeds. Badass Logistics runs the entire project with one accountable crew, so nothing falls through the cracks between the rigger, the carrier, and the installer.

We map the path of travel and floor loadings at both sites, tear down and label, haul oversized machines on permitted routes, and rig and set every machine back to spec in the new layout — including the precision CNC machines that have to come back online holding tolerance.

New to planning a facility move? Start with our plant relocation checklist, then see how we handle the tricky pieces — moving a CNC machine and re-leveling machines to spec at the new site.

how it works

How a plant move runs

  1. Survey & sequenceBoth sites measured; the order every machine comes down and goes back up is planned before teardown.
  2. Disconnect & teardownCoordinated lockout, OEM prep, label-and-map so reassembly isn't archaeology.
  3. Sequenced transportAir-ride and heavy haul, permitted where oversized, in the order the new floor needs them.
  4. Reinstall in the new layoutRigged, set, leveled, and squared to spec — line by line.
  5. Recommission & hand offA floor that's ready to run, on the schedule we agreed.

Plant relocation FAQ

How do you keep downtime short on a plant move?
The whole project is planned before teardown — path of travel, floor loads, machine sequence, and a schedule built around your production calendar so the line is down only for the move window.
Can you relocate a full production line?
Yes — single lines, machine cells, and complete facility relocations, including disconnect, labeling, sequenced transport, reinstallation, and re-leveling to spec.
Do you handle the oversized machines and permits?
Yes — oversized machines are rigged, permitted, and hauled in-house on compliant routes, no hand-off.
