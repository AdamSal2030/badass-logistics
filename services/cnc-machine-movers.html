Machine-tool moving, done like the precision job it is

The fastest way to ruin a machine tool is to move it like furniture. Lift from the wrong casting point, leave an axis free to slide, or skip the re-level and the machine powers on fine and never holds tolerance again. Badass Logistics treats every CNC move as an engineering job: a documented plan, lifting only from the manufacturer's designated points, and a machine that's squared back to spec before we hand it over.

From a single VMC across the shop to a full CNC machine relocation across state lines, you get one accountable crew — and because we run industrial rigging and heavy haul in-house, oversized machines get permitted and hauled without a hand-off.

why it's different

Lift points, not pry bars

CNC machines have designated lift and jacking points in the manual — and only those. Pry under the sheet metal or sling it wrong and you twist the machine's geometry. We pull the lift data first, protect the ways, lock the axes, and move on air skates and air-ride so the casting arrives as straight as it left.

how it works

How a CNC move runs

  1. Survey & lift dataBoth floors measured; weights, lift points, and floor loading pulled from the manual — not guessed.
  2. Power-down & secureQualified disconnect, coolant/air drained, axes and spindle locked to transport position with OEM bracing.
  3. Rig outToe jacks, machine skates or air casters, gantry or crane from the rated points — on a planned path.
  4. Air-ride transportTarped or crated, shock-monitored, routed to avoid rough roads.
  5. Set, level & squarePlaced on the new pad and leveled to the builder's spec, ready for OEM ramp-up and first cut.

CNC machine moving FAQ

How much does it cost to move a CNC machine?
It depends on machine weight and class, rigging access at both ends, distance, and how much disconnect and re-level work is involved. A knee mill across town is a different job than a 40,000-lb horizontal across the country — send the model and both floor layouts for a real number. See our CNC moving guide →
Can you move my whole machine shop?
Yes — single machines, multi-machine cells, and full shop relocations, sequenced around your production so the floor is down only for the move window.
Do you re-level the machine after transit?
Always. The machine is set and leveled and squared to the manufacturer's spec before hand-off; geometry and repeatability depend on it.
