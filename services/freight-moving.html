questions

Freight Moving FAQ

What kinds of freight do you move?
FTL, LTL, partial and volume, drayage, expedited/hot shot, and intermodal — on flatbed, step deck, reefer, dry van, and specialized trailers, including oversized and overweight freight.
What's the difference between FTL, LTL, and partial?
FTL is a full trailer dedicated to one shipment — fastest and most direct. LTL shares trailer space and is cost-effective for smaller loads. Partial sits in between: dedicated space without paying for a whole truck you don't fill.
Do you handle drayage and port containers?
Yes — containers off the port or rail ramp to the warehouse, plant, or next leg, plus the first and last mile of intermodal moves.
Can you move oversized or overweight freight?
That's our specialty. Oversized and heavy freight is backed by our in-house rigging and heavy haul teams, so it stays with one accountable crew. See heavy haul →
